# Arbiter / agentic trading application positioning

Use this as a session-specific reference for accelerator-style answers about Arbiter.

## Product positioning

Arbiter is an agentic decision system for Indian retail traders. It is not a stock tips app and not an auto-trader. It is a decision layer between a trader and their next trade.

Core line:

> Put every trade on trial.

## Problem thesis

Retail traders do not only lose because they lack information. They often struggle because of:

- tip chasing
- FOMO entries
- overtrading
- vague risk and invalidation
- emotional execution
- no post-trade review loop

Most tools push more signals, charts, and activity. Arbiter adds friction before bad trades and structure before entry.

## Product workflow

- Trader submits symbol/setup/timeframe/thesis/risk.
- AI agents debate bull case, bear case, market context, risk, and trader behavior.
- System produces a decision card: enter, wait, or avoid, with reasoning and audit trail.
- After market close, Arbiter compares the outcome against the original thesis so the trader learns from process quality, not just P&L.

> Arbiter is an agentic decision system for Indian retail traders.
