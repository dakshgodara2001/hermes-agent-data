# Accelerator Application Writing

Archived during umbrella consolidation into `startup-mvp-launch`.

## When to use

Use this when writing or tightening answers for accelerator applications, grants, pitch forms, startup competitions, or founder profiles. Especially relevant for prompts about relevant experience, startup description, team, why now, why this founder/team, what has been built, and what problem is being solved.

## Core approach

If the application is about Arbiter / the agentic trading decision product, read `references/arbiter-application-positioning.md` before drafting.

1. Answer the exact prompt first. Do not write generic startup prose.
2. Respect word limits aggressively and state the word count.
3. Lead with strongest proof: track record and wins, not only insight.
4. Use concrete nouns: shipped products, users served, systems built, roles, workflows, evidence.
5. Avoid hype and legal risk. Do not imply guaranteed returns, proven alpha, private data access, or regulated advice unless explicitly true and safe.
6. Make the startup feel earned by connecting founder background to the problem.
7. Offer one best version first; optional variants can follow.

## Wins angle checklist

For “Relevant experience” and similar prompts, include at least two of: shipped 0→1 products, scaled/launched measurable workflow, built systems for a named user group or count, reduced support/ops burden, founder/startup experience, domain exposure explaining why the startup exists.

## Team question pattern

If the founder is solo, be honest and make it sound intentional. Explain why the founder has the right intersection for the wedge, mention real feedback sources, and acknowledge missing depth without sounding weak. Do not invent advisors, cofounders, traction, or named experts.

## Startup description pattern

1. One-line product definition.
2. Specific problem with current market/default behavior.
3. What the product does in workflow terms.
4. Why this is different from generic tools.
5. Concrete output or habit loop.

## Voice and style

Direct, founder-like, specific, high-conviction but not inflated. Prefer active verbs: built, shipped, launched, reduced, scaled, observed, validated.

## Pitfalls

- Do not over-index on insight and forget wins when the question asks for track record.
- Do not say “AI for trading” in a way that sounds like stock tips or easy money.
- Do not use long preambles when the user needs paste-ready application copy.
- Do not overclaim metrics or domain proof.
